Our Committees for ETMUN 2025

ETMUN offers a diverse range of committees to cater to different interests and experience levels. Each committee focuses on specific global issues and provides delegates with the opportunity to engage in meaningful debate and negotiation. Below are the committees available for the upcoming conference:

General Assembly

Level: Beginner to Intermediate

The General Assembly is the main deliberative body of the United Nations, where all member states have equal representation. It discusses a wide range of international issues within the scope of the UN Charter.

Topics:

  • Addressing the Global Refugee Crisis
  • Sustainable Development and Climate Action

Security Council

Level: Intermediate to Advanced

The Security Council is responsible for maintaining international peace and security. It has the power to establish peacekeeping operations, impose sanctions, and authorize military action.

Topics:

  • Conflict Resolution in the Middle East
  • Addressing Threats of Nuclear Proliferation

Human Rights Council

Level: Intermediate

The Human Rights Council is responsible for promoting and protecting human rights around the world. It addresses situations of human rights violations and makes recommendations.

Topics:

  • Rights of Indigenous Peoples
  • Freedom of Expression in the Digital Age

Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC)

Level: Beginner to Intermediate

ECOSOC is responsible for coordinating the economic and social work of the United Nations. It addresses economic, social, and environmental challenges.

Topics:

  • Global Economic Recovery Post-Pandemic
  • Reducing Inequality Within and Among Countries

World Health Organization (WHO)

Level: Beginner to Intermediate

The WHO is the specialized agency of the United Nations responsible for international public health. It works to promote health, keep the world safe, and serve the vulnerable.

Topics:

  • Global Pandemic Preparedness and Response
  • Mental Health Crisis in the Modern World

Crisis Committee

Level: Advanced

The Crisis Committee simulates fast-paced, dynamic scenarios that require quick thinking and adaptability. Delegates respond to evolving crises and make decisions under pressure.

Committee Structure and Procedures

Each committee follows standard Model UN procedures, including formal debate, moderated and unmoderated caucuses, and resolution drafting. Committees are led by experienced chairs who guide the proceedings and ensure adherence to rules of procedure.

Daily Schedule

A typical committee day follows this schedule:

  • 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM: Morning Session I
  • 10:30 AM - 10:45 AM: Break
  • 10:45 AM - 12:15 PM: Morning Session II
  • 12:15 PM - 1:30 PM: Lunch Break
  • 1:30 PM - 3:00 PM: Afternoon Session I
  • 3:00 PM - 3:15 PM: Break
  • 3:15 PM - 4:45 PM: Afternoon Session II

Awards

Each committee will recognize outstanding delegates with the following awards:

  • Best Delegate: Awarded to the delegate who demonstrates exceptional diplomacy, leadership, and contribution to the committee.
  • Outstanding Delegate: Awarded to delegates who show remarkable skills and active participation.
  • Honorable Mention: Awarded to delegates who make notable contributions to the committee.
  • Best Position Paper: Awarded to the delegate who submits the most well-researched and well-written position paper.

Preparation Resources

To help delegates prepare for their committees, we provide the following resources:

  • Study Guides: Comprehensive guides for each committee topic, including background information, key questions, and suggested research areas.
  • Rules of Procedure: Detailed explanation of the rules and procedures followed in ETMUN committees.
  • Position Paper Guidelines: Instructions and templates for writing effective position papers.
  • Research Resources: Recommended sources for researching committee topics and country positions.

All resources will be made available to registered delegates through our online platform. Additionally, we will host pre-conference workshops to help delegates prepare for their committees.
